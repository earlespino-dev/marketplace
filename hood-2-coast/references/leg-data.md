# HTC 2026 — Leg data (all 36 legs)

Transcribed from the 2026 HTC Handbook course-ratings table (p.34) and the individual leg
pages (p.37–77). For the full exchange map, GPS coordinates, turn-by-turn directions,
provisions, and parking notes, read the leg's page in
`${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/2026-HTC-Handbook.pdf`.

**Difficulty:** E = Easy · M = Moderate · H = Hard · VH = Very Hard.
**Net** = total gain minus total loss (negative = net downhill). Both gain and loss matter —
a leg can net downhill while still having significant climbs, or vice versa.

**Runner → legs (12-person team):** runner N runs legs **N, N+12, N+24** (e.g. runner 8 →
legs 8, 20, 32). This holds only for 12-person teams; 8- and 10-person teams follow the
handbook rotation table (p.16) — see the htc-logistics skill.

**Night legs:** any leg run between 6:00pm and 7:00am requires full reflective gear (see
htc-rules). Which legs are run at night depends on the team's assigned start time, not the leg
number — compute it from the schedule, don't assume.

## Notable legs

- **Steepest descents (quad-eccentric / downhill technique):** legs 1 (-2,036), 2 (-1,553),
  30 (-501), 36 (-291). The first three legs off Mt. Hood drop ~1,300–2,000 ft each — going
  out too hard here wrecks your 2nd and 3rd legs.
- **Biggest climbs (hill strength):** leg 20 (+590, 912 ft gain), leg 18 (+223), leg 5 (+218),
  leg 35 (+212), leg 28 (+169), leg 19 (+141), leg 29 (+100, on 602 ft of gain).
- **Hardest-rated (VH):** legs 1, 5, 19, 20, 29.
- **Longest:** legs 14 (7.91), 33 (7.72), 6 (7.10), 35 (7.07).
- **Gravel / dusty:** legs 20, 21 (bandana recommended), plus gravel stretches on 35 and 36.
