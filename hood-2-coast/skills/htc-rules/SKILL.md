# HTC Race Rules

Answers rules questions accurately. Rules carry time penalties or disqualification, so when the
exact wording matters, read it from `${CLAUDE_PLUGIN_ROOT}/references/2026-HTC-Handbook.pdf`
(race rules p.22–23, safety p.27, penalties form p.85, Cut & Run p.32–33) rather than relying
on the summary below.

## Required night safety gear (STRICTLY ENFORCED)

From **6:00pm to 7:00am**, every participant running on the course must:

- wear one **front and one back LED flasher**,
- carry a flashlight, wear a headlamp, or wear a hat headlight, and
- wear a **reflective vest** — and the vest must stay on **until 9:00am**.

Team minimum equipment: 2 reflective vests, 2 LED flashers, 2 flashlights/headlamps. Support
personnel are urged to wear a vest + flasher whenever outside the van on course. Vans may **not**
follow behind their runner. Contactless check-in: photograph all safety gear in the HTC app for
admin approval before your start time.

## Local-team volunteer requirement

Any team with a member within **100 miles of Portland is a "local" team** and must provide
**3 volunteers**, or the team is **disqualified**. (3,600 volunteers support the ~197-mile
course; this is non-negotiable for local teams. Non-local teams are exempt.) Volunteers watch
required training and pass a short questionnaire; job selection opens at a set time — be online
right when it opens to get a good assignment.

## Common penalties (handbook p.22–23 — read for the full list)

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| No stopping to drop off a runner / no parking within 500 ft prior to an exchange | 30 min |
| Vehicle following behind a participant | 30 min |
| Not displaying the two team van signs (front + back windows) | 60 min |
| On-course conduct / quiet hours 10:00pm–6:00am (no loud noise/music) | 30 min |
| Traveling on the left | 60 min |
| Grossly inaccurate race-pace survey times | 60 min |
| Disobeying a Race Official (volunteers, ODOT, traffic safety are all officials) | 60 min / DQ |
| Adding an alternate after the team has started | 60 min |

**Immediate disqualification:** unregistered runner on race day; alcohol/marijuana/drugs on
course; littering, urinating, or defecating on private property; marking public property
(including spray chalk). Race Officials may DQ on the spot for serious violations. A violation
reported by two separate teams and deemed valid can mean automatic DQ.

## Start time, registration, exchanges

- **Start times** are assigned in a Friday **2:00am–2:00pm** window from the team pace survey
  (faster teams earlier). Violating your assigned start time = disqualification + possible
  future suspension.
- Every race-day runner must be officially registered; adds/subs via Team Clubhouse or the HTC
  app up to race day.
- **Van exchanges** happen only at major exchanges 6, 12, 18, 24, 30; the active van always
  carries 6 racers. Runners must run in rotation the entire race.

## Cut & Run

For teams running behind the cutoff schedule, Cut & Run lets officials advance a team to stay on
course (handbook p.32–33). If asked, read those pages for the exact current procedure rather
than summarizing from memory, since the thresholds can change year to year.

## Emergency procedure

Call 911, then find the Exchange Leader or Radio Operator at the nearest exchange (ham operators
are at every exchange zone). Write emergency contact + medical info on the back of race bibs.
Carry a charged phone; AirTags are recommended for tracking teammates in no-coverage areas.
